# Self-evaluation

As our group did not managed to build our system in the time we had, it is hard to say if we did a good job or not.</br>
But we managed to deliver a design, which we think is the best based on our research work. The design choices that we made, were based on:
- our analysis of the problem
- the functions and subfunctions of our system
- a morphological table with all the possible solutions to the functions
- a Kesselringen table, that confirmed the best design to choose </br>

As we had this done, we needed to make the design we choosen, the best that we could have. Therefore, we have made calculations about the cooling and heating time, the pressure that we need on the plate to be pressed and the material flow, so that we know how much we could produce.</br>
After this, we have summurized our work in a CAD model, to have a virtual image of our machine. We also made a PLC program, capable of sychronizing and regulating the process with the extruder.</br>
We did most of the prvious searching work and brainstorming togheter in many group meetings and after we all felt confortable with the subject, everyone got parts of the project to work on individualy.
So, this was a little overview on how we worked during this project. We also had a weekly meeting with our custumer.

## Arne
In this group project, I worked with three people who did not have the same background as I did. Although I had worked in multidisciplinary groups before, this experience was different because I was not working with other mechanical engineers whom I already knew.</br>

At the start of the project, I assumed that my teammates would follow the same design steps that I was familiar with. However, I quickly realized that they had never worked on a project like this before. This created a challenge for me, as I had to explain many aspects of the design process to them. Doing this required significantly more effort than simply completing the work on my own. At times, I felt that if I had not explained or assisted, certain tasks might not have been completed.</br>

On the other hand, my teammates also brought valuable skills that I did not yet possess, particularly in electronics. Even though they were unfamiliar with this specific design methodology, we were able to discuss ideas, debate design choices, and explain our reasoning to one another. This helped us develop better solutions as a team.</br>

Overall, the group dynamic was very positive. We worked well together, communicated clearly, and made solid agreements. No one avoided contributing to the project, which I appreciated, as this is not always the case in group work.</br>

## Esmee
At the start of the project, I expected the mayority of the time to be spent on implementing automation solutions. In hindsight, approximately 80-90% of the project time was spent on theoratical research, analysing the problem, and validating potential solutions that could realistically work. This phase taught me that solution development is often far more complex and uncertain than anticipated at first.</br>

In previous projects, I was used to working with clearly defined problems, where the main challenges typically involved doing correct  calculations and selecting suitable components. While this background provided a solid technical foundation for me, this project needed a different approache that I was not very familiar with yet. This project taught me the key to a succesfull suiting solution is to first develop a deep understanding of the problem itself and why it exists. Before determining what that solution should be or even thinking about requirements. I learned that underlying issues that are not immediately visible can emerge during this problem definition phase. Being able to find the gory details of a problem and proffesionally substantiate solutions using structured methods, such as the Kesselring method, is for me a valuable lesson from this project.</br>

A hard challenge was realising the knowledge gap I had regarding the early phases of projects. I have a strong background in electronics, circuit design, and power systems; however, I realised that these skills are mostly used in the final phase of a project, once the problem and solution direction have been clearly defined. During the early stages of this project, I found that I lacked sufficient knowledge in other disciplines like mechanical design and thermoplastic behaviour, which made it more difficult to contribute with confidence. Recognising this gap was challenging, but it also thaught me the use of interdiciplanary projects. </br>

For me the group dynamic was really great, eventhough we did not always agree, everyone really wanted to put effort in and wanted to contribute.
